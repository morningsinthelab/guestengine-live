He Built The First All-Digital Studio & Changed Music Forever

If you've ever worked your ass off and wondered if the dream is slipping further away, you're not alone. You do the work. You stay disciplined. And still, there's that quiet question, am I actually building towards something real?

Today, we're meeting David, someone who's lived inside that question and kept moving. As a child, David survived eye cancer. That experience reshaped his world early, and music became his anchor. By 12, he was playing professional gigs in Boston clubs.

While most kids were still discovering their interests, at 16, David started a music technology business, not a hobby, a real operation. That path led him to become Aerosmith's in-house recording engineer and technologist for over 13 years. He helped design studios, engineer records, and solve problems when the pressure was high and the stakes were real. He also helped build the first all-Pro Tools studio used to record Ricky Martin's Livin' La Vida Loca.

Today, David works with artists and creators who want control over their craft, their tools, and the life they're building. He believes the dream isn't impossible. It just requires clarity, patience, and showing up long after motivation fades. Today, we're talking ambition, passion, technology, and the grit it takes to build something that actually lasts.

David, we're excited to have you on Mornings in the Lab.
